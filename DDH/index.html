Welcome!

Title description, June 7, 2018

Hey little peeps, wait till you see what I found in the garden today! Ya know how we’ve been talkin’ about all the different layers that make a garden, right? And this month we’ve been chattin about dirt. Come on now, have you ever seen a garden without dirt? No way! Maybe you think you should clean up the garden, because it’s full of dirt. But if it wasn’t for what under and around the plants, they just couldn’t grow. The thing is, not all dirt is good dirt. Well today I want to tell you about composting.

Did you ever look really close at dirt? It doesn’t look like something plants would grow in does it? It’s kinda lifeless—just a pile of gritty brown stuff. But you can change all that and make a kind of dirt plants love by doing a few things. First, you rake up some dead leaves.

Then, you can add some left overs that were going in the trash anyway. Ask your parents to help you save scraps from the table. So, already you helped clean up the yard and reused some leftovers instead of throwing them out. You’re SO cool!

Now you add a few things, like some water (not too much) and then stir it up every few days. If you do it right, the worms that live underground will find this pile of unusable throw away stuff and start to eat it.

After a few weeks, the worms break it down into something called compost. That’s dirt that has a lot of organic stuff (stuff plant life is made of) in it. It smells way cool and looks and feels totally different from just plain old dirt. And plants love IT!

There’s more to it than that, but you get the idea. If you want to learn more about dirt and compost, write to me and ask for your free how-to-compost at home kit and I will send it to you. If we both surround the plants with a hug from compost, they will grow big and strong. It works for veggies, flowers, bushes and trees—just about anything. Stay tuned for more about worms when I blog a whole series about bugs in the garden. Until then, remember, Digger Digs YOU!
